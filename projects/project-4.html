Learning Platform Redesign

Making online education more engaging and accessible

Role Product Designer & Researcher
Timeline 10 Months
Team 8 People

The Problem

A major online learning platform with 500K+ students was experiencing high course dropout rates. Only 12% of students completed courses they started. Interviews revealed that the interface felt overwhelming, progress was hard to track, and students didn't know how to navigate their learning journey.

Key pain points identified:

  • Course structure was unclear; students didn't know what to study next
  • No sense of progress or achievement
  • Accessibility issues made learning difficult for users with disabilities
  • Overwhelming course catalog with poor search and filtering

Goals & Constraints

Primary Goal

Increase course completion rate from 12% to 50%

Secondary Goal

Meet WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards

Constraint 1

Migrate 10K+ existing courses without data loss

Constraint 2

Support legacy systems and integrations

Design Process

Research Phase

Led a comprehensive research initiative:

  • 25 learner interviews across different demographics and skill levels
  • 5,000+ survey responses about learning goals and frustrations
  • Analysis of 100+ courses to identify teaching patterns
  • Accessibility audit revealing WCAG violations

Key Insights

Research revealed that learners want:

  • Clear roadmaps showing where they are and what's next
  • Visible progress that feels motivating, not stressful
  • Simplified interface that doesn't feel like a content dump

Design & Testing

Created wireframes and prototypes testing different approaches to course structure, progress tracking, and navigation. Conducted moderated usability testing with 12 learners at different stages of their journey. Tested with assistive technologies (screen readers, magnifiers) to ensure accessibility.

Solution

1. Learning Pathways

Redesigned course navigation to show clear learning paths with chapters and lessons organized logically. Students see the full structure upfront and always know where they are. Suggested "next steps" at the bottom of each lesson.

2. Progress Visualization

Implemented multiple progress indicators without being overwhelming: course-level progress bar, lesson checkmarks, and achievement badges. Progress feels rewarding, not stressful.

3. Accessible by Design

Built the entire interface with accessibility at the core:

  • Proper semantic HTML with ARIA labels
  • High contrast color combinations (7:1 ratio)
  • Keyboard-only navigation fully supported
  • Captions and transcripts for all video content
  • Tested extensively with screen readers

4. Improved Discoverability

Redesigned the course catalog with smarter filtering, better search, and personalized recommendations based on learning history and goals. Course cards now show prerequisites and estimated completion time.

Impact on Business

The redesign had significant business impact: completion rate increased from 12% to 65% (a 5.4x improvement), course enrollment grew 45%, and student satisfaction scores improved significantly. The platform became a leader in accessible online education.

User Feedback

"This platform actually feels like it wants to help me learn. The interface got out of the way and I just focused on the content. I've completed 5 courses now—previously I couldn't finish one." — Maria Rodriguez, Learner

Key Learnings

  • In educational products, clear structure and progress visualization drive completion
  • Accessibility benefits everyone—learners with disabilities were our canaries
  • Comprehensive research early prevents costly redesigns later
  • Small UX improvements can have massive impact on engagement metrics
  • Users value simplicity over feature richness in learning contexts
